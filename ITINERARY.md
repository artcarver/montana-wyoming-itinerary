## Day-by-Day Itinerary

### Day 1 — Saturday, August 8 — Fly In to Glacier
*Los Angeles → Glacier*

> Land in Bozeman, stock up, and make the long, scenic drive north to the edge of Glacier.

**Drive:** 5h 15m / 312 mi · **Sun:** Rise 6:15 / Set 8:54
**🏨 Tonight — Night 1 of 2:** Mini Golden Inns — Hungry Horse, MT · conf `73478801173025` · $475 · check-in 4–9 PM / checkout 11 AM

- **6:00 AM — Los Angeles (LAX)** _[Travel]_
  Check in and clear security for a nonstop Delta flight to Bozeman.
  Nonstop this year — DL3947 departs 8:00 AM and lands Bozeman 11:40 AM (2h 40m). You gain an hour crossing into Mountain Time. Confirmation GSDBVY.
- **11:40 AM — Arrive Bozeman (BZN)** _[Travel]_
  Small airport — rental counters are right in the terminal.
  You should be on the road within 30–40 minutes of landing. Mountain Time from here on.
- **12:45 PM — Lunch + Grocery Run** _[Food]_
  Downtown Bozeman Main Street, 10 min from the airport, then stock up for the next two days.
  Pickle Barrel, Feed Cafe, or any Main Street spot. Stop at Town & Country Foods or Albertsons for breakfast items, sandwiches, snacks, and water for the next two days.
- **2:15 PM — Madison Buffalo Jump** _[Viewpoint]_  _(▸ 45 min from Bozeman)_
  A cliff where Native Americans drove bison herds for thousands of years.
  45 min west of Bozeman off I-90. Short walk to the overlook with interpretive signs and valley views. 15–20 min stop, small $6 park fee.
- **2:45 PM — Missouri Headwaters** _[Viewpoint]_
  Where the Jefferson, Madison, and Gallatin rivers merge to form the Missouri.
  Lewis and Clark camped here in 1805. Short walk to the confluence point, picnic tables if you want to stretch. 15–20 min.
- **6:30 PM — Mini Golden Inns — Check In** _[Lodging]_
  Hungry Horse, just outside the west entrance. Settle in and unload groceries.
  Check-in is 4–9 PM with no 24hr desk — call ahead if past 9 PM.
- **8:00 PM — Lake McDonald Sunset** _[Viewpoint]_  _(▸ 15 min to Apgar)_
  First taste of Glacier — Apgar Village shoreline in golden light.
  West Glacier entrance is 15 min away. Walk the rocky beach at Apgar; the famous colorful rocks are scattered along the shore. Sunset around 8:50 PM. Drive up toward the lodge for glassy evening reflections.
- **10:30 PM — Night Sky — Aurora Watch at Apgar** _[Night Sky]_
  48°N in one of the darkest places in the lower 48 — the best shot at aurora you’ll have on this trip.
  Darkness falls around 10:30 PM. Stay at the Apgar shoreline — Lake McDonald faces north and the mountains frame the sky. Your phone camera will pick up a greenish glow before your naked eye does — point it north regardless of how ordinary the sky looks. Download SpaceWeatherLive before you leave and set Kp alerts. Experts put the real visibility threshold at Kp 5–6 at 48°N — so wait for that before staying up past midnight. Solar Cycle 25 is still active in 2026, which is the main reason this is genuinely worth watching.

### Day 2 — Sunday, August 9 — Glacier West Side
*Glacier NP*

> Old-growth cedars, turquoise gorges, and the gradual climb to Avalanche Lake — the headline hike.

**Drive:** ~1.5h in park · **On foot:** ~9 mi if you do it all · **Sun:** Rise 6:17 / Set 8:52
**Wildlife:** Mountain goat, Moose
**🏨 Tonight — Night 2 of 2:** Mini Golden Inns — Hungry Horse, MT · conf `73478801173025` · Incl. · check-in 4–9 PM / checkout 11 AM

- **8:00 AM — Enter West Glacier** _[Viewpoint]_
  Buy the America the Beautiful pass here — it covers every park on the trip.
  $80 annual pass covers Glacier, Yellowstone, and Grand Teton. Get to Avalanche before 9:30 AM — the lot fills fast and stays full till late afternoon.
- **8:25 AM — Lake McDonald Lodge** _[Viewpoint]_
  Historic Swiss chalet lodge with a massive stone fireplace, right on the lake.
  Walk the dark-wood lobby, then down to the shore where red, green, and blue rocks are visible through the clear water. 20–30 min.
- **9:00 AM — Johns Lake Loop** _[Trail]_  _(▲ 2 mi / flat)_
  Easy 2-mile forest loop — a gentle warm-up before the big hike.
  Flat, well-maintained, and far less crowded than Avalanche. Views of Johns Lake and the surrounding peaks. 45 min–1 hr.
- **10:00 AM — Trail of the Cedars** _[Trail]_  _(▲ 1 mi loop / flat)_
  Flat 1-mile boardwalk through ancient red cedar and hemlock forest.
  Towering old-growth trees, ferns everywhere, and a gorge with turquoise water carving through rock. Completely flat and accessible. Don’t rush it.
- **10:45 AM — Avalanche Lake** _[Trail]_  _(▲ 5.8 mi RT / gradual)_
  The main event — a mountain lake walled by cliffs streaming with waterfalls.
  A gradual 5.8-mile round trip; the 500 ft of gain is spread over nearly 3 miles so it never feels steep. Sit at the lake a while when you arrive — the setting is spectacular.
- **3:00 PM — Apgar Village** _[Viewpoint]_
  Visitor center, lakeshore, gift shop, and a well-earned ice cream.
  Browse the displays and walk the Apgar shore. Glacier Park Boat Co. runs ~1.5 hr Lake McDonald cruises from the dock if you want time on the water.
- **8:00 PM — Lake McDonald Sunset** _[Viewpoint]_
  Last chance on the west side — glassy water and mountain reflections.
  Tomorrow you cross to the east side and won’t be back. If you caught it last night, skip and rest — tomorrow is Going-to-the-Sun Road and you start early.
- **10:30 PM — Night Sky — Aurora Watch at Lake McDonald** _[Night Sky]_
  Second chance — but tomorrow is Going-to-the-Sun Road, so be selective about staying up.
  Check SpaceWeatherLive before heading out. Kp 5 or higher is the real threshold for aurora at 48°N — below that, get sleep. Logan Pass parking fills by 9 AM and you want an early start. If the forecast is good, the Apgar shore is worth it: Lake McDonald reflects the aurora when active, doubling the whole display. Either way, step outside for a few minutes — the Milky Way alone at this latitude is something SoCal simply doesn’t offer.

### Day 3 — Monday, August 10 — Going-to-the-Sun Road
*Going-to-the-Sun Road*

> Fifty miles of road carved into the mountainside, over the Continental Divide at Logan Pass.

**Drive:** ~2.5h / 50 mi · **On foot:** 5–7 mi · **Sun:** Rise 6:18 / Set 8:50
**Wildlife:** Mountain goat, Moose
**🏨 Tonight:** St. Mary Village — St. Mary, MT — east entrance · conf `73478287602240` · $409 · check-in 4 PM / checkout 11 AM

- **8:30 AM — The Loop** _[Viewpoint]_
  Big switchback where the road starts climbing in earnest — all new territory.
  Pullout with views down the valley you just drove. From here the road narrows, clings to the mountainside, and the views open dramatically.
- **8:45 AM — Weeping Wall** _[Viewpoint]_
  Water cascades down the rock face directly onto the road — you drive through it.
  Lighter flow in August than spring but still there. Bird Woman Falls Overlook and Big Bend follow close behind — both quick, stunning pullouts.
- **9:15 AM — Logan Pass** _[Viewpoint]_  _(▸ 24 min from The Loop)_
  The top. 6,646 ft, the Continental Divide, and the trailheads for the two best walks.
  Arrive by 9:15 for a parking shot — if the lot is full, wait it out. Small visitor center and restrooms.
- **9:30 AM — Hidden Lake Overlook** _[Trail]_  _(▲ 2.7 mi RT / 500 ft)_
  Boardwalk-then-dirt climb to an overlook above Hidden Lake, ringed by peaks.
  Mountain goats are commonly spotted — scan the meadows and rocks. Stop at the ridge overlook; don’t descend to the lake itself (steep climb back). Bearhat Mountain rises beyond.
- **11:00 AM — Highline Trail** _[Trail]_  _(▲ 1–2 mi RT / flat)_
  A flat traverse carved into a cliff face with the Garden Wall above.
  Sheer drop below, a bolted cable at the narrow start if heights unsettle you. Walk even 15–20 min out for a view you won’t find anywhere else, then turn around.
- **12:05 PM — Jackson Glacier Overlook** _[Viewpoint]_
  One of the easiest glaciers to see without hiking — the park’s 7th largest.
  Roadside pullout with interpretive signs. Sunrift Gorge follows with a 5-min walk to a slot canyon.
- **12:45 PM — Sun Point** _[Viewpoint]_
  A rocky point jutting into St. Mary Lake with wide mountain views.
  Optional flat lakeshore walk to Baring Falls (1.2 mi RT). Wind can be strong out on the point.
- **1:15 PM — St. Mary Falls** _[Trail]_  _(▲ 1.7 mi RT / stairs)_
  Short forest descent to a powerful waterfall with a turquoise pool.
  1.7-mile round trip with some stairs. Add 1.4 mi to reach Virginia Falls — even bigger and more dramatic — if your legs are up for it.
- **2:15 PM — Wild Goose Island** _[Viewpoint]_
  The iconic shot — a tiny island in St. Mary Lake backed by mountains.
  Roadside pullout. Pull over, take the photo, keep going. Rising Sun area nearby has a store and boat tours.
- **3:30 PM — Drive to Many Glacier** _[Scenic Drive]_  _(▸ 35 min)_
  Head north to the Many Glacier valley for the evening boat tour.
  About 35 min from the St. Mary area. You’ll check into St. Mary Village afterward this evening — no need to stop first.
- **4:15 PM — Many Glacier Hotel Deck** _[Viewpoint]_
  Pick up your tickets, then walk the Swiftcurrent shore to the historic lodge deck.
  Tickets at the dock directly behind Many Glacier Hotel — arrive 30 min before departure. The lakeside deck is one of the best lodge views anywhere while you wait for the boat.
- **5:00 PM — Many Glacier Boat Tour** _[On the Water]_  _(▲ boat ~1.5 hrs)_
  Guided cruise across Swiftcurrent Lake and Lake Josephine — the trip’s once-in-a-lifetime boat ride.
  Glacier Park Boat Co. runs two boats with a short guided walk between the lakes; peaks close in from every side. ~1.5 hrs. Be at the ticket office behind Many Glacier Hotel by 4:30 to claim your seats.
- **6:45 PM — St. Mary Village — Check In + Dinner** _[Lodging]_
  Drive back to St. Mary, check in, and an easy evening.
  On-site restaurant and general store. An evening lakeshore walk at Rising Sun catches the east-side light as the sun drops behind the peaks.
- **10:30 PM — Night Sky — Aurora Watch at St. Mary Lake** _[Night Sky]_
  Your last night in aurora territory — and experts agree the east side’s open prairie horizon is the best of your three Glacier nights for it.
  Walk to the Rising Sun boat ramp or the St. Mary Lake overlook and face north. No mountains cut the horizon here — open prairie all the way toward Canada. After tonight the trip heads south where aurora needs Kp 7+ to be visible. The real threshold at 48°N is Kp 5–6 — if the forecast hits that and skies are clear, this is the one to stay up for. After tonight you won’t be at this latitude again.

### Day 4 — Tuesday, August 11 — Many Glacier + Drive South
*Glacier → Yellowstone*

> The most dramatic valley in Glacier, then the longest drive of the trip — south to Yellowstone’s door.

**Drive:** ~7.5h — longest day · **On foot:** 3–5 mi · **Sun:** Rise 6:17 / Set 8:37
**Wildlife:** Moose
**🏨 Tonight:** Yellowstone Basin Inn — Gardiner, MT — north gate · conf `BE-70789` · $291 · check-in 4–9 PM / checkout 10 AM · breakfast included

- **7:50 AM — Arrive Many Glacier** _[Viewpoint]_  _(▸ 35 min from St. Mary)_
  The most dramatic valley in the park — peaks closing in on all sides.
  Park near the Many Glacier Hotel or Swiftcurrent Motor Inn. You’ll see why people call this the best area in Glacier.
- **8:00 AM — Fishercap Lake** _[Trail]_  _(▲ 0.6 mi RT / flat)_
  A flat 0.3-mile walk to one of the best moose-spotting lakes in the park.
  Moose frequently feed in the marshy edges in early morning. Even without one, the peaks behind the lake are beautiful. 15–20 min.
- **8:30 AM — Swiftcurrent Lake Stroll** _[Trail]_  _(▲ up to 2.5 mi / flat)_
  A flat morning walk along the lake you cruised last night — peaks doubled in still water.
  An easy shoreline loop around Swiftcurrent Lake (or just walk out as far as you like), with the Many Glacier Hotel across the water. Quiet and gorgeous in the morning. Start the long drive south by ~9:30.
- **12:05 PM — Rocky Mountain Front Drive** _[Scenic Drive]_  _(▸ ~6+ hrs total to Gardiner)_
  US-89 south along a wall of peaks rising straight from the prairie.
  Through the Blackfeet Reservation — the Museum of the Plains Indian is in Browning if you want a 20–30 min stop. One of the most striking geological transitions in the lower 48: plains, then a wall of rock with no foothills.
- **2:00 PM — Choteau** _[Food]_
  Small town at the foot of the Rockies — lunch and dinosaur fossils.
  A few Main Street spots for lunch. The Old Trail Museum has dinosaurs from the Two Medicine Formation nearby. 30–45 min.
- **5:00 PM — Livingston** _[Town]_
  A gritty, authentic western town on the Yellowstone River.
  Walk Main Street, grab a coffee, stretch. Dan Bailey’s Fly Shop is an institution. 15–20 min.
- **5:30 PM — Paradise Valley** _[Scenic Drive]_
  US-89 follows the Yellowstone River south — Absaroka and Gallatin ranges framing it.
  One of the most scenic stretches of highway in Montana. Watch for elk and deer in the fields. About an hour to Gardiner.
- **6:45 PM — Gardiner + Roosevelt Arch** _[Lodging]_
  Yellowstone’s original 1903 north entrance — you’ve arrived.
  Check into Yellowstone Basin Inn (free homemade breakfast). Walk to the stone arch inscribed “For the Benefit and Enjoyment of the People.” If you have energy, preview Mammoth Hot Springs — 5 min into the park, travertine glowing at sunset, elk on the lawns.
- **10:00 PM — Night Sky — Milky Way over Mammoth** _[Night Sky]_
  Elk on the lawns, thermal steam rising in the dark, and a Bortle 2–4 sky that SoCal simply cannot offer.
  Drive five minutes back into the park to Mammoth after dinner. Face south — that’s where the galactic core sits in mid-August. The Milky Way here rates Bortle 2–4, which means individual dark dust lanes are visible to the naked eye — not a smear, an actual structure. Elk graze the hotel lawns in complete indifference. Steam drifts silently off the travertine terraces. The best Milky Way window is 1–2 AM when the core is highest. Bring a jacket — temperatures drop fast at this elevation.

### Day 5 — Wednesday, August 12 — Yellowstone North & East
*Yellowstone NP*

> Wildlife at dawn, otherworldly travertine terraces, and the Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone.

**Drive:** ~5.5h in park · **On foot:** 3–5 mi · **Sun:** Rise 6:15 / Set 8:36
**Wildlife:** Bison, Gray wolf, Grizzly bear, Pronghorn
**🏨 Tonight — Night 1 of 2:** Yellowstone West Gate Hotel — West Yellowstone, MT · conf `73478829989296` · $588 · check-in 4 PM / checkout 11 AM · breakfast included

- **6:00 AM — Lamar Valley at Dawn** _[Wildlife]_  _(▸ 1h 15m from Gardiner)_
  The best wildlife viewing in the lower 48 — the optional but unforgettable early start.
  Bison herds everywhere, pronghorn in the sage, and with luck wolves on the ridgelines and grizzlies in the meadows. Bring binoculars; look for clusters of spotting scopes and ask what they’re watching. Best window 6:00–9:00 AM. (Sleep-in option: start at Mammoth at 8 AM instead.)
- **9:45 AM — Petrified Tree** _[Viewpoint]_
  A standing petrified redwood — 50 million years old, turned to stone.
  Short dead-end road west of Tower Junction; 10-min walk from parking. Calcite Springs Overlook (basalt columns, steam vents) and Tower Fall (132-ft drop through volcanic spires) follow close by.
- **10:45 AM — Mammoth Hot Springs** _[Geothermal]_  _(▲ ~2 mi boardwalk)_
  Otherworldly white-and-orange travertine terraces built over thousands of years.
  Walk the Upper Terraces boardwalk loop (~1.5 mi) and the shorter Lower Terraces. The formations change constantly — some active last year may be dry now. Elk are almost always on the lawns by the hotel.
- **1:00 PM — Norris Geyser Basin** _[Geothermal]_  _(▲ ~2 mi boardwalk)_
  The hottest, most dynamic thermal area in the park — two boardwalk loops.
  Porcelain Basin is an eerie white landscape of steaming vents; Back Basin is forested and holds Steamboat, the world’s tallest active geyser (erupts unpredictably). Budget an hour for both.
- **2:30 PM — Grand Canyon of the Yellowstone** _[Viewpoint]_  _(▲ 1–3 mi / viewpoints)_
  A 1,200-ft canyon stained yellow and orange, with two thundering waterfalls.
  Artist Point is the famous one — Lower Falls (308 ft, twice the height of Niagara) framed down the canyon. Add Lookout Point, Uncle Tom’s 328 steps, or the Brink of the Lower Falls trail if your legs allow.
- **4:00 PM — Hayden Valley** _[Wildlife]_
  A vast open meadow along the river — hundreds of bison, sometimes grizzlies.
  Drive slowly, pull over at the designated turnouts, and scan. Impressive even from the car. Don’t approach — bison look slow but run 35 mph.
- **5:45 PM — West Gate Hotel — Check In** _[Lodging]_
  Two nights in West Yellowstone — no repacking tomorrow.
  Pool and hot tub to soak your legs after a massive day. Walk to dinner — Madison Crossing, Firehole, or Pete’s Pizza are all close.
- **10:30 PM — Night Sky — Perseid Meteor Shower Peak** _[Night Sky]_
  2026 is one of the best Perseid years in a decade — new moon on Aug 12 means zero lunar interference at peak.
  Expert peak: August 13, 2026 at around 7 AM Mountain Time, with the best viewing the nights of Aug 12 and 13. ZHR up to 100 meteors per hour under ideal conditions — the new moon makes 2026 exceptionally dark. Step out of the hotel and walk half a block east toward the park; that’s all it takes. Meteors radiate from the northeast but streak all across the sky. Somewhere in that darkness: bison, elk, probably wolves. The park goes completely quiet and you won’t hear a thing.

### Day 6 — Thursday, August 13 — Yellowstone Geyser Loop
*Yellowstone NP*

> The recovery day — geysers, rainbow hot springs, and bubbling mud, almost all on flat boardwalks.

**Drive:** ~3.5h in park · **On foot:** 6–8 mi / mostly flat · **Sun:** Rise 6:19 / Set 8:35
**🏨 Tonight — Night 2 of 2:** Yellowstone West Gate Hotel — West Yellowstone, MT · conf `73478829989296` · Incl. · check-in 4 PM / checkout 11 AM · breakfast included

- **9:40 AM — Firehole Canyon Drive** _[Viewpoint]_
  A one-way loop along the Firehole River — falls and a thermal swimming hole.
  Firehole Falls drops ~40 ft right by the road. Further along, the river is naturally heated and people actually swim in August. Turquoise water in a canyon — worth the 10-min detour.
- **10:00 AM — Fountain Paint Pot** _[Geothermal]_  _(▲ 0.5 mi loop)_
  All four Yellowstone thermal types in one short loop — the bubbling mud pots steal it.
  Geysers, hot springs, mud pots, and fumaroles on a 0.5-mile boardwalk. Firehole Lake Drive nearby has Great Fountain and White Dome geysers visible from the car.
- **11:15 AM — Grand Prismatic Spring** _[Geothermal]_  _(▲ 0.8 mi boardwalk)_
  The largest hot spring in the U.S. — 370 ft of orange, yellow, green, and deep blue.
  The boardwalk takes you right alongside it; steam will partly obscure the view (that’s normal) and you’ll feel the heat. Excelsior Geyser Crater on the same loop pours 4,000 gallons a minute into the river.
- **11:45 AM — Fairy Falls Overlook** _[Trail]_  _(▲ 2 mi RT / short climb)_
  The climb to the iconic overhead view of Grand Prismatic’s full rainbow.
  About a mile of flat gravel then a short hillside to the platform — the photo you’ve seen everywhere. Continue 0.6 mi to Fairy Falls itself if you want the extra mile.
- **12:45 PM — Biscuit Basin** _[Geothermal]_  _(▲ 0.4 mi loop)_
  A short, quieter loop — Sapphire Pool is impossibly blue and clear.
  Black Sand Basin next door adds Emerald Pool and the frequently-erupting Cliff Geyser. 15–20 min each.
- **2:00 PM — Old Faithful** _[Geothermal]_
  The eruption — 130–180 ft every ~90 minutes. Check the predicted time and grab a seat early.
  Eruptions last 2–5 minutes and impress every time. Lunch at the Old Faithful Inn first — a 1904 log landmark with a 65-ft stone fireplace, worth walking through even if you don’t eat.
- **2:15 PM — Upper Geyser Basin Loop** _[Trail]_  _(▲ ~3 mi loop / flat)_
  The densest concentration of geysers on Earth — over 150 in one square mile.
  A 3-mile boardwalk past Geyser Hill, Castle, Grand (tallest predictable, 200 ft), Riverside, and the deep-blue Morning Glory Pool at the far end. The heart of Yellowstone — take your time.
- **5:15 PM — West Thumb Geyser Basin** _[Geothermal]_  _(▲ 0.6 mi boardwalk)_
  Thermal features right on the shore of Yellowstone Lake — including Fishing Cone.
  A lakeside boardwalk where steaming springs meet the vast lake, unlike any other basin. You’ll be back here at sunrise tomorrow for a completely different scene.

### Day 7 — Friday, August 14 — Yellowstone to Grand Teton
*Yellowstone → Grand Teton*

> Drive the empty dawn road south, then a full day in the drive-and-stop paradise of Grand Teton.

**Drive:** ~4h · **On foot:** 3–5 mi · **Sun:** Rise 6:21 / Set 8:36
**Wildlife:** Moose, Elk, Bald eagle
**🏨 Tonight:** Antler Inn — Jackson, WY · conf `73498280187487` · $395 · check-in 3 PM / checkout 11 AM

- **6:45 AM — West Thumb at Sunrise** _[Geothermal]_  _(▸ 1h 45m from West Yellowstone)_
  You saw it yesterday — at dawn it’s a different place entirely.
  Steam pours thick off every pool in the cold morning air, the lake is glassy, the light low and warm. If any early alarm pays off, this is it.
- **8:15 AM — Colter Bay Village** _[Trail]_  _(▲ 2 mi loop / flat)_
  First Grand Teton stop — Jackson Lake with the Teton Range across the water.
  Walk the flat 2-mile lakeshore loop. The visitor center has a notable collection of Native American art and artifacts.
- **9:15 AM — Jackson Lake Lodge** _[Viewpoint]_
  60-ft picture windows framing the Teton Range like a painting.
  Pull in, grab a coffee, and stand at the windows. The view alone is worth the stop. Willow Flats Overlook just south is a reliable morning moose spot.
- **9:30 AM — Oxbow Bend** _[Viewpoint]_
  One of the most photographed bends anywhere — Mount Moran mirrored in still water.
  Morning is best, when the Snake River is calmest. Roadside pullout, 5–10 min.
- **9:45 AM — Signal Mountain Summit** _[Scenic Drive]_  _(▸ 5 mi up / 5 mi down)_
  A 5-mile road to a 360° panorama — the widest Teton view with no hiking.
  Tetons, Jackson Lake, the Snake River valley, and the Gros Ventre range to the east. Two viewpoints at the top. 30–45 min round trip.
- **10:45 AM — Jenny Lake + Hidden Falls** _[Trail]_  _(▲ ~1.5 mi RT / boat)_
  Boat shuttle across the lake to a powerful waterfall and Inspiration Point.
  A 12-min shuttle, then ~1 mile of trail (some stairs on the Inspiration Point section) to a wide view back over Jenny Lake. The whole loop runs about 1.5 hrs including the boat.
- **1:45 PM — Snake River Overlook** _[Viewpoint]_
  Ansel Adams’ famous composition — the river winding beneath the range.
  Roadside pullout on the main highway. The shot that made the Tetons iconic. Quick stop.
- **2:30 PM — Lunch at Dornan’s, Moose** _[Food]_
  A relaxed riverside lunch right where the float launches.
  Dornan’s Pizza & Pasta Co. (or the Chuckwagon) sits at Moose with a deck facing the Tetons — and it’s steps from the float put-in, so there’s no rush before your trip. Take the afternoon slow.
- **5:00 PM — Snake River Scenic Float** _[On the Water]_  _(▲ boat ~2 hrs)_
  Solitude Float Trips — 10 miles of calm water with the full range as backdrop, in warm low light.
  No rapids, just a guided scenic float — eagles, ospreys, moose, and elk are common, and late-day light is the best of the day. ~2–2.5 hrs on the water, off the river ~7:15 PM. The 5 PM is the last departure; book ahead at 307-733-2871.
- **7:45 PM — Jackson Town Square** _[Town]_
  Elk-antler arches at every corner — the best dinner scene of the trip.
  Antlers shed at the nearby National Elk Refuge. Check into the Antler Inn (walkable from the square), then dinner: Hatch tacos, Liberty Burgers, Thai Me Up, or Snake River Brewing. You’ll catch Schwabacher and Mormon Row at dawn tomorrow.
- **10:00 PM — Night Sky — Milky Way over the Tetons** _[Night Sky]_
  Teton County became a Dark Sky Community in April 2026 — the first county in the US to earn that designation.
  Drive ten minutes north to Antelope Flats / Mormon Row and face northwest toward the peaks — Jackson’s light dome is behind you to the south. The T.A.
